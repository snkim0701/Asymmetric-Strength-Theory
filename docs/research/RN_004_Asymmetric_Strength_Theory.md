# RN_004 : Asymmetric Strength Theory
### Version 0.1
### HDSDM Research Note

---

# 1. Motivation

Most technical indicators describe how the price has moved.

Examples include:

- RSI
- MACD
- MFI
- Bollinger Bands

However, they rarely answer a more fundamental question.

> Why do some stocks refuse to fall despite persistent selling pressure?

This research attempts to explain that phenomenon.

---

# 2. Economic Logic

Financial markets are driven by the interaction between buyers and sellers.

Price is not the cause.

Price is merely the consequence.

Therefore,

Observed Variables

    Price
    Volume
    Trading Flow

should be regarded as observations of an underlying hidden state.

That hidden state is called

    Strength

which cannot be observed directly.

---

# 3. Common Sense

CS1

Strong stocks attract buying during corrections.

Market participants often describe this as

- Buying on dips
- Sell-off has been absorbed
- Strong downside support

CS2

Structural growth industries tend to maintain stronger downside support.

Example

HBM Industry

Despite

- Memory cycle concerns
- CAPEX slowdown concerns
- Valuation concerns

the market often absorbs selling pressure.

CS3

Average acquisition cost influences investors' behavior.

When the market price approaches the average acquisition cost,

buying interest is likely to increase.

Therefore,

Support is not merely a price level.

Support represents accumulated market commitment.

---

# 4. Definitions

Average Cost

Estimated average acquisition cost of market participants.

Support Strength

The ability of the market to defend a price region.

Absorption

The ability to absorb selling pressure.

Asymmetric Strength

A latent market state representing persistent buying power.

---

# 5. Research Hypotheses

HP1

Average Cost contributes to Support Strength.

HP2

Higher Support Strength leads to stronger Absorption.

HP3

Higher Absorption predicts higher future returns.

HP4

Structural growth industries exhibit stronger Absorption than mature industries.
